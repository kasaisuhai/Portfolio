MoodChart.me

I had the idea for this project because I'm interested in things like journaling and meditating, and I knew that this could be very helpful for a lot of people because so many of us just go through life unaware of what we're feeling. My team and I created MoodChart.me because we wanted to help our users keep track of their overall mood. Moods can last for hours while emotions last anywhere from seconds to minutes, at most. This is why it's typically easier to identify emotional triggers but difficult to identify triggers for moods.

Research

Starting off we created a proto-persona to get an idea of who our user would be. We knew our user would be someone who is either mental health conscious, or someone with a mood disorder such as Bipolar Disorder, Depression, etc.

After getting an idea of who our user is, we created a survey. We mainly wanted to find out how aware people are of their moods and what other factors might be correlated to said moods. We also asked about average amount of sleep and energy levels. We found that 50.1% of respondents rated awareness of mood as 6 or less, 31.3% described their overall mood as anxious, 62.6 rated their energy level as 6 or less, and 25% reported sleeping less than 6 hours on average.

Our next piece of research was a competitor analysis. We decided to analyze two competitors, eMoodTracker and MoodTracker. With eMoodTracker, their design was outdated and they had limited features. One of their biggest strengths were safety features that protected their users important data and ensured it isn't misused. As for MoodTracker, their design was also outdated and they didn't cater to a broad range of users. Their strengths were allowing users to share their data with their own wellness team and a community space where users could find like-minded individuals.

After the competitor analysis, we created a value proposition matrix to find a place for our product in the market. Most of our competitors were using a subscription model, and with so many subscription services out there, we didn't want to saddle our users with yet another subscription. We also wanted to create something that was more accessible and easy-to-use.

Definition & Ideation

At this point, we had enough information to start defining and ideating. We decided to start by defining the problem and hypothesizing a solution. The problem is that people are typically unaware of their mood, which causes them to go through life being numb to what they are feeling and unable to process it. We hypothesized that by tracking their moods and possible contributing factors, users can use that data to better understand their feelings and better process them.

Meet Aubrie. Aubrie is a yoga instructor and a mother to a 2-year old. Aubrie takes her mental health very seriously. She loves to meditate and write out her thoughts but often doesn't have the time throughout the day. Aubrie needs a simple online mood tracking solution that she can fill out at any time.

We created a user flow with simplicity in mind. We wanted it to be easy for users to hop onto the website, log an entry, and carry on with their day. We also wanted users to be able to check their analytics and view the history of their entries. We also designed the style guide with different color themes in mind, as well as a light and dark mode. We picked out different emojies and icons to use that we thought would accurately convey different emotions to our users.

Prototyping & Testing

The first step here was creating a low-fi wireframe to decide how we wanted to structure the website. It helped us to figure out which elements we would need in our design and how to incorporate them into the layout.

After this, we moved on to creating a hi-fi prototype. We added a color theme, icons and graphs, and inputs for tracking mood.

Once we finished the hi-fi prototype, we conducted usability tests. We assigned testers with three tasks: Enter a new mood, view mood entry history, and view account information. We had a 100% success rate with the last two, and a 92% success rate with the first task. This meant our design was intuitive enough that users could easily perform the basic functions.

Development

Once we tested our design, we could move on to development. I was in charge of most of the development on this project. We used github for the repository, and bootstrap for most of the components. We decided on a file structure and organized everything in a way that made development go smoothly. Once we set that up, I made 4 pages and built them out based on our design. There were a few minor things that had to be changed since we were using bootstrap, but we remained faithful to the original design.

Conclusion

During the process of this project, we built a whole front-end product in 3 weeks. It was equal parts rewarding and challenging. By designing and developing this website, I came to fully understand the importance of cultivating awareness of ones mood. Awareness is the first step in creating any change, and for people who are unhappy with their current life, this site will bring users awareness and allow them to change.
